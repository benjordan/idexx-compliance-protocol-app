{{Pet Name}}'s Test Results

No Heartworm Detected!

We didn't find any evidence of heartworms today. Heartworms are parasites that are transmitted by when a mosquito bites, and the adult worms live in your dog's heart and the blood vessels of the lungs.

Symptoms may include persistent cough, fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance, decreased appetite and weight loss.

No Lyme Detected!

We didn't find any evidence of Lyme infection today. Lyme disease is a bacterial infection transmitted by black-legged ticks (deer ticks).

While many pets can have a Lyme infection and show no symptoms, the most common ones seen in dogs include fever, decreased appetite, swollen/painful joints, limping, and lethargy.

No Anaplasmosis Detected!

We didn't find any evidence of Anaplasmosis today. This is another infection that is transmitted by ticks (in this case black-legged/deer ticks and brown dog ticks).

Symptoms look a lot like Lyme and may include lack of energy, limping, swollen or painful joints, and loss of appetite.

No Ehrlichiosis Detected!

We didn't find any evidence of Ehrlichiosis today. Ehrlichiosis is a bacterial infection transmitted by ticks (specifically, brown dog ticks and lone star ticks).

Symptoms look a lot like Lyme and may include lack of energy, limping, swollen or painful joints, and loss of appetite.
